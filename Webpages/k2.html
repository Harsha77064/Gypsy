5 Day Trip

Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country," is a captivating state located in the southwestern region of India. Known for its scenic beauty, cultural heritage, and rich biodiversity, Kerala has become a popular tourist destination worldwide. Whether you seek tranquility in its backwaters, adventure in its wildlife reserves, or a glimpse into its vibrant traditions, Kerala promises an unforgettable journey for every visitor.


Whether you're strolling through the tea gardens of Munnar, cruising along the backwaters of Alleppey, or simply sipping on a freshly brewed cup of local tea, Kerala will weave its magic around you, leaving you with memories to cherish forever.
Come, experience the charm of Kerala, where nature's beauty and heartfelt warmth blend seamlessly to create an unforgettable journey of a lifetime.

| Plan |


Day 1: Arrival in Kerala

Upon arrival in Kerala, our guide will meet you at the airport or railway station and escort you to hotel. After some rest, you will be visiting sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple ,later which you can enjoy Kerala's authentic cuisine.



Day 2: Lake View

After breakfast, we will take you to visit beautiful Veli Lake, one of the most popular attractions in Kerala. You can enjoy the mesmerizing view of the scenery. Later in the day, you can visit malls such as the one in Travancore,Lulu International Mall etc.

Day 3: Palace Visit

You will then be visiting other renowned attractions such as the Kanakakkunnu Palace,which showcases exquisite architectural styles, blending elements of traditional Kerala and British colonial influences.


Day 4:Light House,ahoy!

Next on tour, is the Vizhinjam Lighthouse which is a prominent landmark located in the coastal town of Vizhinjam, providing a panoramic view of the Arabian Sea. you can enjoy the beauty of nature along with sea food near the light house.



Day 5: Napier Museum

Followed by a tour of the Napier Museum which showcases a wide range of art and historical artifacts, including ancient sculptures, bronze idols, coins, and ivory carvings and offers a captivating glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of Kerala
